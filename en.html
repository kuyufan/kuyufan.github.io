About

Bio

I am Ku-Yu Fan, a PhD student in Mathematics at Nagoya University, advised by Hidekazu Furusho. My research centers on multiple zeta values and their algebraic and analytic structures, including the motivic version of Yamamoto’s integral, coproduct formulas, Schur multiple zeta values, arborifications of multiple zeta values, and p-adic multiple zeta values, multiple polylogarithms, and L-functions. I am open to discussion and collaboration.

Patents

  • Tiling Unit Element Structure and Tiling Monomer Element Structure with Multiple Combination StylesGranted
    2021 Inventors: Ku-Yu Fan, Wen-Liang Hung Taiwan patent link

    Summary: This utility model presents a tiling unit element structure obtained by cutting a truncated octahedron. Its tiling hexagonal faces connect to tiling quadrilateral faces. A closed curve is formed across the six hexagonal faces of the truncated octahedron and passes through the midpoints of the edges that connect adjacent hexagons. Rays drawn from the geometric center to points on the closed curve determine cutting planes, dividing the truncated octahedron into two three-dimensional structures so that the unit becomes half of a truncated octahedron. This allows both combination and tiling properties and yields many structures with rich variation, supporting spatial geometry learning, structural organization, and creativity through educational play.

  • Rhombic Dodecahedron Puzzle and Poly-Rhombic Dodecahedron PuzzleGranted
    2018 Inventors: Ku-Yu Fan, Wen-Liang Hung Taiwan patent link United States and International link one United States and International link two

    Summary: This utility model describes a poly-rhombic dodecahedron puzzle composed of multiple blocks, forming a three-dimensional body equivalent to several rhombic dodecahedra connected together. Each block consists of two identical unit elements. Each unit has several faces that are rhombi or triangles; adjacent triangular faces form a concave shape, and the faces enclose a closed space. With these special blocks, one can assemble many different poly-rhombic dodecahedra. It suits all ages, helps build spatial ability and mental rotation, and improves strategies for solving geometric problems while keeping the activity playful.

  • Hexagonal Prism Educational PuzzleGranted
    2017 Inventors: Ku-Yu Fan, Wen-Liang Hung Taiwan patent link United States and International link Product introduction video

    Summary: This work presents a hexagonal prism puzzle with 18 different block shapes. Each block is assembled from several unit elements, and many blocks together form a hexagonal prismatic solid. The set of unit elements contains two types, where the first unit has a larger volume than the second. There is no unique solution or specific method required. The puzzle develops spatial sense, deepens understanding of geometric figures, encourages multiple lines of thought, and makes learning enjoyable.
